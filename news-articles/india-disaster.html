India's Disaster Response Mechanism18th January, 2017


KM Singh, Vice President, PPF delivered a talk on 18th January, 2017 on ’India's Disaster Response Mechanism' to a group of officers of National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) undergoing one week training at the Indian Institute of Mass Communication, New Delhi. In his talk the background and objective of constitution of NDRF was covered and also the initial constraints in setting up this force. Referring to  the role and unique features of this force, it was mentioned that in a short span of only 10 years NDRF has earned a niche for its professionalism as a multi-disciplinary, multi-skilled, high-tech force capable of handling any natural disaster, besides chemical, biological, nuclear and radiological emergencies within the country and abroad. This force has also acquired the unique distinction of being the single largest dedicated disaster response force in the world.

It was mentioned in the talk that while the NDRF has come up well at the national level, there is a need to have a multi-tier response mechanism in the country with State Disaster Response Force (SDRF) at state level, Civil Defence, Fire Services and Home Guards at district levels and NGOs and community volunteers at grass root levels as an integral part of disaster response mechanism.
